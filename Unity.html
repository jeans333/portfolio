Overview

There is a constant struggle to juggle school work with personal life. Finding balance in hustle culture is a continuous learning curve for everyone, especially for students who are adjusting to workloads or managing schedules.

Despite the numerous applications to help with time management and planning, we saw an opportunity to create an app that encourages a healthier digital lifestyle with students and professionals. With an emphasis on productivity and well being, Omniflow offers a custom-tailored experience for every user and their priorities.

Problem Space

We noticed that many students tend to be intimidated or discouraged from using workspace platforms. Staying on top of deadlines and completing daily to-do lists can be difficult, let alone spending an extensive amount of time to navigate a time-management app

This contradiction led us to wonder

How can we design an app that helps users develop a personal workspace to stay organized with tasks and goals while balancing wellness?Â

Primary Research - User Interviews

We continued with primary research by conducting user interviews, including Alumni and working individuals, First and upper year University students. The leading questions looked into their current organiztoin methods and habits to discover user needs and painpoints

Brought up the struggle of Balance between work and personal life with Time as a common keyword

Depend on some form of online application to organize tasks and deadlines

Frustrations

  • Multiple apps: many of the same apps with similar features can feel redundant
  • Social life: missing out on social events
  • Time-management: Procrastinating with social media distractions

Motivators

  • Time: having time for leisure activities
  • Connecting with peers: sense of community in learning
  • Academic validation: feeling of success from progress in school

Why invest time in tracking tasks if it takes longer than doing them?

Many applications can overlook the factor of time when simply focusing on delivery of various features. We continued with our design processes with this in consideration

Research takeaways

Fear of missing out

Users struggle with focusing on either school or social life without feeling guilty about how they are spending time. Students from other campuses feel more isolated or less connected with peers

Going back to old ways

Time management applications with various customization features can be overwhelming for users to set up and get adjusted to, often causing them to give up after a short period of time

Neglect of self care

When interviewing students, most said self care was not even on the list of priorities during school terms. Does self care and work only be mutually exclusive?

With this in consideration, we had brainstorming sessions to develop features that would facilitate time management and connection with peers

Features to include

  • Wellness feature: with reminders and encouraging prompts to track ones progress of tasks to stay on top of personal wellness goals
  • Study spot tracker: to help students save time on finding spaces to study on campus
  • customize notifications: frequency for tasks based on their urgency or adjust notifications for when they would like to be reminded less
  • connect page: for peers to keep users motivated without fear of missing out as you can meet people with similar goals
prioritizing long term use with set up difficulty so users are not overwhelmed and waste time trying to learn something new, so users dont easily go back to old habits that may not be effective
