Overview

There is a constant struggle to juggle school work with personal life. Finding balance in hustle culture is a continuous learning curve for everyone, especially for students who are adjusting to workloads or managing schedules.

Despite the numerous applications to help with time management and planning, we saw an opportunity to create an app that encourages a healthier digital lifestyle with students and professionals. With an emphasis on productivity and well being, Omniflow offers a custom-tailored experience for every user and their priorities.


View Final Design

Problem Space

We noticed that many students tend to be intimidated or discouraged from using workspace platforms. Staying on top of deadlines and completing daily to-do lists can be difficult, let alone spending an extensive amount of time to navigate a time-management app

This contradiction led us to wonder

How can we develop a task management app that adapts to students' unique needs without adding complexity challenges?

Secondary Research - Competitive Analysis

To get a better understanding of the commonly used applications, we wanted to see where we could improve through a competitive analysis

Notion

  • Combines to-do lists, planning and calendar features all on one platform with many customization options
  • Clean minimalist design
  • Overwhelming and significant learning curve

Google Calendar

  • Easy to use and organize upcoming tasks
  • Lacks flexibility in task management
  • Limited note taking and documentation features

Primary Research - User Interviews

We continued with primary research by conducting user interviews with alumni, freshman, and upper year University students. The questions were directed to their current organizational methods and habits to discover ways in which we can help maximize productivity.

Brought up the struggle of Balance between work and personal life with Time as a common keyword

Depend on some form of online application to organize tasks and deadlines

Frustrations

  • Multiple apps: Many of the same apps with similar features can feel redundant
  • Social life: Missing out on social events due to academics
  • Time-management: Procrastinating with social media distractions

Motivators

  • Time: Having time for personal interests and hobbies
  • Connecting with peers: Feeling a sense of community when studying with others
  • Academic validation: Motivation to work harder after seeing results

Why invest time in tracking tasks if it takes longer than doing them?

Many applications can overlook the factor of time when simply focusing on delivery of various features. We continued with our design processes with this in consideration

User Archetypes

With the research findings, we created archetypes to better empathize with our user insights and how we could address concerns that many platforms may overlook.

Summer - The Overwhelmed Freshman

"I want to excel in my studies and enjoy my university life, but finding the right balance is harder than I expected."

Frustrations

  • Time management skills used in high-school are not as effective
  • Volume of assignments and deadlines
  • Feeling like she's missing out because she hears about events after they happen

Alex - The Stressed Senior

"I'm trying my best to keep up with everything, but I just need some space to breathe and not worry about deadlines and shifts."

Frustrations

  • Burnout from over commitment with a part-time job
  • Lack of time for personal interests and relaxation
  • Ineffective stress management, impacting overall health and academic focus

Research takeaways

Fear of missing out

Users can't focus on school or social life without feeling guilty about how they are spending time. Students from other campuses feel more isolated or less connected with peers.

Going back to old ways

Time management applications with various customization features can be overwhelming for users to set up and get adjusted to, often causing them to give up after a short period of time.

Neglect of self care

When interviewing students, most said self care was not even on the list of priorities during school terms. Does self care and work have to be mutually exclusive?

With this in consideration, we had brainstorming sessions to develop features that would facilitate time management and connection with peers.

Features to include

  • Wellness feature: Reminders and encouraging prompts to track ones progress of tasks to stay on top of personal wellness goals
  • Study spot tracker: Map of available campus spots to help students save time on finding spaces to study
  • Custom notifications: Select frequency of notifications based on task load and when they'd like to take breaks.
  • Connect page: Allowing peers to keep users motivated without fear of missing out as you can meet people with similar goals
We realized prioritizing an easy set up process was important, so users are not overwhelmed and go back to old habits that may not be effective.

Usability Testing

We led usability testing among students with our mid-fidelity prototypes to gather valuable insights. Utilizing the feedback to inform our targeted improvements including the following major changes.

Dashboard - Before

We discovered the need to edit and add widgets barely occurred in the later parts of a users journey and it was simply blocking space on their dashboard. The placement also made it easier to accidentally click on it with our mobile prototype. When users were asked to rank the priorities of the default widgets, responsive were very subjective and it was difficult to meet all needs.

“I mainly found the floating action buttons useful in the beginning to add/edit widgets when I was setting up”

Dashboard - After

Begin with an empty dashboard to customize with selected widgets, enhancing intuitiveness in dashboard setup and widget feature.

Choose relevant widgets from separate tab to meet unique student needs and save time navigating and updating the default widgets.

New Widget Tab

By removing the floating button saves space on dashboard, students can explore and understand their functions through detailed overviews.
